What Brickify does

Brickify lets you disable selected apps on a device until a passcode and second factor are entered. On iPhone this uses Apple's Screen Time (Family Controls) framework; on Android it uses the Accessibility Service and Device Administrator permissions. Brickify is intended to be set up by the person responsible for the device — typically a parent, guardian, or the device's own adult owner using it for self-control.

Appropriate use

  • You must be the owner of the device, or have the clear authority of the device's owner (e.g. a parent setting up a child's phone), to install and configure Brickify on it.
  • Brickify is not intended for covert surveillance of another adult without their knowledge and consent.
  • You are responsible for keeping your passcode, second factor, and recovery codes secure, and for who you share them with.

Emergency calling

Brickify never blocks emergency calls. The Emergency Call button on the bricked screen dials emergency services regardless of brick status, on both platforms.

No guarantee of enforcement

Brickify relies on operating-system permissions (Screen Time / Family Controls on iOS, Accessibility Service and Device Administrator on Android) that the device owner can revoke, and on protections that a factory reset, app deletion, or OS-level workaround can bypass — see the in-app Help section for details on both platforms. Brickify is a supportive tool, not an unbypassable lock, and we don't guarantee it will prevent access to blocked apps in every circumstance.
