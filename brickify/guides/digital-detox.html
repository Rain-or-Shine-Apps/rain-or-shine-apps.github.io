Set up your own phone for a digital detox

The problem with "I'll just not open the app" is that it doesn't work. Brickify puts a real second person between you and the apps you can't self-regulate around.

← All guides

Unlike a parent locking a child's phone, this is you locking your own — on purpose, with a friend or partner holding the piece you don't have. That's the whole mechanism: willpower doesn't have to hold at 11pm if unlocking genuinely isn't up to you alone.

Setup

1

Choose a PIN — then hand off the second factor

During setup, pick an authenticator app or email code as your second factor. Then give that piece to someone you trust: install the authenticator on their phone, or use an email address only they can access. Knowing your own PIN isn't enough to get back in without them.

2

Block the apps that actually get you

Be specific rather than blocking everything — a phone with no apps at all just gets deleted or ignored. Target the two or three you genuinely lose hours to.

3

Decide: always-on, or scheduled?

Two ways to run this — pick whichever matches the problem:

Always blocked

Leave your profile as the default with no schedule. Tap BRICK when you're ready to commit. Only your second-factor holder can undo it — there's no "just for five minutes."

Blocked at the times you actually struggle

e.g. midnight–7am for late-night scrolling. Create a second profile scheduled for that window with the block list on it; keep your default profile open the rest of the day.

For the scheduled version: set the profile up like an overnight window — start time 00:00, end time 07:00, every day (or just the nights that are actually a problem). Brickify treats an overnight window as normal; it doesn't need to be "all day" to wrap past midnight.

Tap BRICK once to arm it, and it repeats every night on its own from then on — no re-arming required.

Worth doing too

  • Screen Time / Device Admin — set this up so you can't just delete the app at 1am to get around it. That defeats the entire point.
  • Recovery codes — write them down and store them somewhere your future, tempted self won't think to look, but you'll find in a genuine emergency.
  • Be honest about scope — block what you actually struggle with. A profile that blocks everything tends to get abandoned within a week; one that blocks the two apps that matter tends to stick.
